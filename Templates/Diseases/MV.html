Tomato Mosaic Virus (ToMV)

About: Tomato Mosaic Virus (ToMV) is a viral disease that affects tomato plants, causing mosaic patterns on leaves, stunted growth, leaf deformation, and discolored fruits. It is transmitted through infected seeds, plants, or mechanical means.

Symptoms: Symptoms of ToMV include mosaic patterns (light and dark green areas) on tomato leaves, stunted growth, and leaf distortion. Infected fruits may show discoloration, mottling, or uneven ripening. The virus can weaken plants and reduce overall yield.

Prevention:

  • Use certified virus-free seeds or transplants from reputable sources.
  • Control aphids and other insect vectors that can transmit the virus.
  • Remove and destroy infected plants promptly to prevent further spread.
  • Avoid tobacco exposure near tomato plants, as tobacco can harbor and transmit ToMV.
  • Plant resistant tomato varieties if available in your area.
  • Practice crop rotation with non-host plants to break the disease cycle.
